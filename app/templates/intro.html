Analytical Engine

7 Methods. One Complete Picture.

Every analysis method applied to the 239,368-record Jordan Civil Defense dataset, from raw coordinate conversion to AI-assisted station placement.

📊
Historical Analysis
Year-over-Year Trend Analysis
Full temporal breakdown of 239,368 incidents across 2018–2025. Identifies 2019 as the worst year on record (51,436 fires), tracks growth rates, regional shifts, and seasonal distribution patterns using bar, stacked, and donut charts.
Dataset: 239,368 records · 8 years · 3 regions
🌦️
Climate Intelligence
Pearson Climate Correlation
Calculates Pearson correlation coefficients between annual fire volumes and three climate variables — max temperature, rainfall (mm), and wind speed (km/h) — revealing that rainfall has the strongest statistical link to fire frequency (r = 0.79).
Key finding: Rainfall r=0.79 · Wind r=-0.597 · Temp r=-0.383
🔮
Predictive AI
Polynomial Regression (Degree 2)
Trained on 8 years of regional fire data per governorate, this model captures the non-linear acceleration and plateau patterns that linear regression misses. Projects fire incident volumes for 2026–2028 with confidence bands per region.
Forecast horizon: 2026 · 2027 · 2028 per region
🗺️
K-Means ML
K-Means Station Optimization
Scikit-Learn K-Means clustering iteratively calculates the mathematical center of gravity for fire incident clusters across Jordan. Proposes optimal Civil Defense station placements that minimize average response distance, compared against the 48 real station locations.
Result: AI-optimized placement vs. 48 real stations
📐
Spatial Analysis
Haversine Distance / Coverage Gap
For each of 2,000 sampled fire incidents, the Haversine formula computes exact great-circle distance to every Civil Defense station. Identifies that 5.3% of fires occur beyond 15km of any station, exposing critical coverage gaps by region.
Coverage: 94.7% within 15km · 5.3% beyond
🎯
Risk Prediction
Multi-Factor Risk Engine
Combines historical incident frequency (40%), climate factor scoring (35%), and regional base risk profiling (25%) into a real-time risk score. Outputs low/medium/high classification with tactical recommendations and resource deployment guidance for Civil Defense commanders.
Factors: Historical 40% · Climate 35% · Regional 25%
📍
Data Engineering
Multi-System Coordinate Conversion
Raw data spanned two incompatible coordinate systems across 8 years — Palestine 1923 Grid (2018–2019) and UTM Zone 36N (2020–2025). Custom transformation pipelines converted all records to standard WGS-84 GPS coordinates for unified geospatial analysis.
Systems unified: Palestine 1923 Grid + UTM Zone 36N → WGS-84
🔭
GIS / Visualization
Density Heatmap & Marker Clustering
Client-side Leaflet.heat renders kernel density estimation heatmaps from raw GPS coordinates with blue→red intensity gradients. MarkerCluster groups individual incidents by zoom level, enabling drill-down from country-level patterns to city-block incident details.
Rendered: 12,000 heatmap points · 8,000 clustered markers
📋
Operational Output
Resource Allocation & Hotspot Report
Synthesizes all analyses into actionable tactical output: Top 5 critical hotspot zones with exact coordinates, fire density rankings, AI-recommended station deployments, SMS alert triggers, and exportable Civil Defense resource briefings.
Output: Hotspot CSV · Tactical briefings · SMS triggers

Methodology

From Raw Data to Actionable Intelligence

The full pipeline from raw Excel sheets with incompatible coordinate systems to real-time risk prediction and K-Means station optimization.

Explore the Map
1
Data Ingestion & Coordinate Transformation

Raw Civil Defense Excel files across 8 years used two incompatible coordinate systems (Palestine 1923 Grid and UTM Zone 36N). Custom parsers converted all 239,368 records to WGS-84 GPS coordinates with Jordan bounds validation.

2
Arabic Type Mapping & Classification

Arabic subtype labels (غابات-وأشجار, حقول-وأعشاب, etc.) were normalized to Forest/Grassland categories. Region strings mapped to Irbid, Amman, and Ma'an governorates. All records batch-loaded into SQLite in 50k chunks.

3
Statistical Analysis & Climate Correlation

Year-over-year trend analysis (bar/stacked charts), Pearson correlations between climate variables and fire volumes, and fire type distribution across all 8 years and 3 regions.

4
K-Means Clustering for Station Optimization

Scikit-Learn K-Means clusters fire incidents geospatially to find mathematical centers of gravity. Compares AI-proposed station placement against the 48 real Civil Defense stations to identify uncovered zones.

5
Polynomial Regression Forecasting (2026–2028)

Degree-2 polynomial regression trained per region captures non-linear acceleration in historical fire counts, projecting future volumes with confidence bounds through 2028.

6
Real-Time Risk Scoring & Tactical Output

Multi-factor engine combines historical frequency, climate scores, and regional risk profiles into a 0–100 risk score with LOW/MEDIUM/HIGH classification, SMS alert triggers, and resource deployment recommendations.
